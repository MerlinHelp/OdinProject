Description

 Al Pastor is a Mexican dish and my favorite kind of taco, and it originates from Mexico City and Puebla. It is a variation of the Middle Eastern dish shawarma. Al Pastor is seasoned and marinated pork and the tacos (that I have seen) contain pineapples, cilantro, onions, lime, and Al Pastor

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • Pork
  • Dried Chiles
  • Tortillas

Marinade Ingredients

  • Onion
  • Garlic Cloves
  • Allspice
  • Peppercorn
  • Cloves
  • Mexican Oregano
  • Cumin
  • Ginger
  • Achiote powder
  • Salt
  • Apple Cider Vinegar
  • Juice

Toppings

  • Chopped Pineapple
  • Onion
  • Cilantro
  • Lime Juice

Steps

Prepare Pork

  1. Remove any large pieces of fat.
  2. Slice the pork horizontally into long and wide, 1/4’’ thick pieces

Soften Chiles

  1. Remove seeds and veins from the dried chiles (wear gloves if desired)
  2. Add them to a small saucepan.
  3. Cover with water, bring to a boil and simmer for 15 minutes, to soften the chiles.

Make Marinade

  1. Add softened chiles and ¼ cup of the water from the pot to:
     High powdered blender with onion, garlic cloves, allspice,
     Peppercorn, cloves, Mexican oregano, cumin, ginger, achiote powder, salt, apple cider vinegar, and juice.
  2. Blend until completely smooth.

Marinate

  1. Add pork and marinade to a large bowl or ziplock bag, mixing to coat the pork all over.
  2. Cover and refrigerate, ideally for several hours, or overnight.

Grill

  1. Remove pork from marinade and cook on a very hot grill for a few minutes on each side until just shy of 145 degrees F.
     Remove to a cutting board to rest for 5 minutes.
     Chop into small pieces for serving. (Grill pineapple if desired)

Assemble

  1. Warm tortillas in a hot dry skillet, or toast them on the grill.
     Add a spoonful of pork.
     Top with chopped pineapple, onion, cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice.

Serve

  1. Enjoy with a side of Mexican Rice, chips and salsa, and a glass of Horchata.
